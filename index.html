Time to Financial Independence (TFI) Calculator

Use this form to understand how long it will take you to achieve Financial Independence. Financial Independence comes when you have enough yield from your savings to cover for your expenses.


You don't need to become a millionaire to achieve financial independence, neither do your need to work till 60 to get there. You also don't need to have a high-growth career. Everyone who can keep their expenses within their means and grow their investments responsibly can achieve financial independence. This calculator will help you understand that.

Annual Salary *
(est) Growth in Annual Salary

The above two fields relate to your current annual salary and the estimated growth in your salary over the years. The natural range for this is between 2.5% (just matching inflation) and 10% (charting a growing career).

Annual Expenses *
(est) Increase in Annual Expenses

The above two fields relate to your current annual expenses and the estimated increase in your expenses over the years. The natural range for this is between 2.5% (just matching inflation) and 10% (expecting significant upgrade in lifestyle).

Current Savings *
(est) RoI on Savings

The field on the left indicates your current savings. Just add up your liquid assets (bank balance, unit trusts, securities). Leave out property that you might be using as primary residence. Property that is used as primary residence won't provide you an yield as you would be living there, but since you are living there, it should be bringing down your expenses reported above.


The field on the right indicates an estimation of how your savings will grow. Bank Balance returns nothing much, unit trusts might return 5-6%, market securities might return 7-8%.

Yield on Savings *

At some point your savings should switch to providing you enough of your expenses at that point of time. When that happens, it should not erode your savings, but just provide you an yield. Yields come from interest, dividend or rent. Ranges vary from between 3% (interest) to about 8% (REITs).

Your TFI would happen in years.
